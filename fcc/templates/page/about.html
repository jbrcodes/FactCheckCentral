This Website

This website, informally called Fact-Check Central, regularly retrieves fact-checking information from a selection of fact-checking websites. The contents of this website are updated several times a week. Clicking on an entry will take you to the corresponding page on the fact-checking site.

The format in which statements and their "truthfulness" ratings are published differs between fact-checking websites. Some will publish the original statement, the source, and the fact-checker's conclusion from a strict number of options, such as: true, false or misleading. Other websites take a less structured approach. The different formats of the excerpts displayed here reflects that variation in formats.

The selected fact-checking websites are visited and checked for new entries using a technique called web scraping. Scraping a web page consists of automating the visiting of the page, without human intervention. This site displays the most recent entries from fact-checking sites.

The IFCN

The International Fact-Checking Network (IFCN) is a creation of the Poynter Institute. The IFCN has developed a code of principles "...for organizations that regularly publish nonpartisan reports on the accuracy of statements by public figures, major institutions, and other widely circulated claims of interest to society."

Signatories of the IFCN code of principles agree to abide by the code, and submit to yearly independent assessments to retain their signatory status. Organizations that appear on this website were chosen from the IFCN list of signatories.
